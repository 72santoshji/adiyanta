The fire was lit.
Not violent. Not calm.
Simply burning.

Wood transformed into smoke, into ash, into air.
But the fire remained only the cause.

This is where Adiyanta begins.
Not with answers.
But with ten questions,
asked before the fire.

About the Author

Prateek Chaudhary is not a seeker by profession,
nor a philosopher by training.

He does not claim answers.
He documents inquiry.

This work belongs to no doctrine,
no school of thought,
and no conclusion.

The fire has done its work.

What was false has burned.

What remains does not need light.
