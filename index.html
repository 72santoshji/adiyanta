ADIYANTA

यत् सर्वं भवति   पर स्वयं न भवति
“That because of which everything happens, yet which itself does not happen.”

That which exists before the beginning
and remains after the end.

This is not a book.
It is a sitting.

Before the Fire — the Chit Became Witness

The fire was lit.
Not violent. Not calm.
Simply burning.

Wood transformed into smoke, into ash, into air.
But the fire remained only the cause.

This is where Adiyanta begins.
Not with answers.
But with ten questions,
asked before the fire. Those questions were not philosophical.
They were not spiritual exercises.

They arose because something did not fit anymore.

Not beliefs.
Not identity.
Not even the idea of “self”.
If you have ever felt:

– restlessness without reason
– silence that felt heavier than noise
– a question that did not want an answer
Then you already know where this fire points.
This book does not explain those questions.

It only shows how they were asked.

About the Author

Prateek Chaudhary is not a seeker by profession,
nor a philosopher by training.

He does not claim answers.
He documents inquiry.

The fire has done its work.

What was false has burned.

What remains does not need light.

If these questions stirred something within you,

allow the silence to continue.
